OPIOIDs

OVERVIEW:

→ Nociceptive pain / Neurogenic pain
→ morphine-like effects
→ mimic the action of endorphines/eknkephalins/dynorphines
→ relieve intense pain
→ abuse because of euphoric properties
→ anatognist can reserve the action of opiods

OPIODS RECEPTORS

→ interact stereospecically
→ μ,κ,δ
→ different specificity
→ analgesic properties of μ & κ receptor (drosal honrs)
→ members of G-protein-coupled receptor family & inhibit adenyly cyclase
→ ions channels ( ↑ K+ / ↓ Ca2+ )


STRONG AGONIST


A.Morphine

1.Mechanism o action
→ receptors on CNS/PNS/GI/Urinary bladder
→ cause: - hyperpolarization of nerve cells

- inhibition of nerve firing & transmitter release (postynaptic)
  • act at κ receptor(laminaI/II at drosal horn) → decrease release of substance P
  • inhibit release of excitatory transmitters
2.Actions
  1. ANALGESIA: -by raising the pain threshold at the spinal chrod level & alerting the brain's perception of pain )
  2. EUPHORIA: -disinhibition of dopamine-containing neuros of the ventral tegmentum
  3. RESPIRATION : - respiratory depression (reducing of the sensitivity of respiratory neurons to CO2)
  4. DEPRESSION OF COUGH : -anti-tussive properties
  5. MIOSIS : - "pinpoint pupil" (due to μ&κ receptor activation)
- enchance parasympathetic simulation of the eye (due to excitment of Edinger-Westphal nucleus)
  1. EMESIS : - vomiting (due to stimulation of chemoreceptor trigger zone in the area postrema )
  2. GIT : - relieves diarrhea & dysentery ( ↑ tone & ↓ motility of intestinal cirular smooth muscle ) // ↑ tone of anal sphincte
- constipation ( use of stool softener "docusate" + stimulant laxative "sena" )
  1. CARDIOVASCULAR : - no major effects ** in large doses Hypotension&Bradycardia may occur
- Resp depr + Co2 retention = dilated cerebral vessels & ↑ CFS → CONTRAINDICATED: on head/brain injury
  1. HISTAMINE RELEASE : - cause urticaria,sweating,vasodilation
- bronchoconstricion CONTRAINDICATED to asthmatics
  1. HORMONAL ACTIONS: - ↑ GH release & enchance prolactin secration
- ↑ antidiuretic hormone = urinary retention
3.Therapeutic uses:
  1. ANALGESIA (as supplament for inducing-sleep properties of Temazepam)
  2. DIARHHEA treat. ( tincture of opium and camphorated tincture of opium (paregoric) have been used to treat diarrhea )
  3. COUGH treat. ( although suppress cough Codein/Dextormethorphan are widely choosen)
  4. ACUTE PULMONARY EDEMA treat. (IV. dramatically relieves dyspnea )
4.Pharmacokinetcs: (linear) - ( predictable+flexible dosaging)
  1. ADMINISTRATION : -first pass metabolism occurs in liver
-absorbtion from GIT is slow + erratic ( P.O admn. extended-release version form to provide consistent plasma level)
  1. DISTRIBUTION : - rapidly enters all body tissues (including fetuses)
-small % crosses blood-brain barrier ( least lipophilic of the common opiods) // fentanyl/methadone more fat-soluble)
  1. FATE: -conjugation with glucuronic acid in the liver
- excreted primarly in urine / lesser on bile
- 4 of 6 hours but much considerably longer when injected epidurally
-elderly more sensitive
5.Adverse effect:
→ severe respiratory depression (avoid or monitor patient with emphysema )
→ vomiting,dysphoria,hypotenison
→ ↑ intracranial pressure
→ enchance cerebral & spinal ishemia
→ acute urinary retention
→ used cautiously - in bronchial asthma, liver failure, impaired renal function
6.Tolerance and Physical dependence:
→ repeated usage produces tolerance
→ physical and psycological dependence oocur withdraw : autonomic,motor,psychological responses detoxification : PO admn of methadone,buprenorphine,clonidine
7.Drug interaction:
→ rare
→ depressant action may enhances by phenothiazines,MAOIs,tricyclic antidepressants

B.Meperidine (synthetic opiod sturctually unrelated to morphine, acute pain )

1.Mechanism of action :
→ μ & κ
2.Actions:
→ depression of respiration similar to morphine
→ no cardiovascular action on PO admn. // IV admin. ↓ peripheral resistance & ↑ peripheral blood flow = ↑ Cardiac rate
→ as morphine ↑ CFS + dilates cerebral vessels + contract smooth muscle to a lesser extent
→ does not cause pinpoint pupil rather dilate it through the anticholinergic action
3.Therapeutic uses:
→ANALGESIA (NOT recomended for long-term use due to its active metabolite "normeperidine" which has neurotoxic properties)
→ does not treat diarrhea or cough
→ ↓ urinary retention than morphine
4.Pharmacokinetics:
→ well absorbed from the GIT ( available of PO admn.)
→ most often admn. parenterally
→ 2 to 4 hrs
→ N-demethylated to "normeperidine" in the liver and exrected in urine.
5.Adverse effects:
→ large/repeatative doses = anxiety,tremor,muslcle twitches // dilate pupil + hyperactive reflexes
→ hypotension (admn. postoperatively)
→ dry mouth & blurr vision (due to antimuscarinic action)
→ depression enouraged with other antipsychotics drugs
→ Beer list ( inappropriate for geriatric / impaired renal function due to accumulation of normeperidine)
→ not revesible by admn. of naloxone
