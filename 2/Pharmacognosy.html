OPIUM

  • air dried milky latex, rapidly getting brown,characteristic odour

  • Dried is hard, fragile, grainy. At 37 0C becomes plastic and sticky

OPIUM ALKALOIDS - UTILIZATION:

→ MORPHINE ←

  • strong analgesic – anodyne / high risk of addiction
  • with atropine (supression of kidney and gallbladder colics)
  • 90% for preparation of semi-synthetics derivatives
• methylmorphine (CODEINE),
  • ethylmorphine (DIOLAN)
  • • antagonists(NALORFINE)
→ HEROIN ←
  • diacetylmorphine
  • strong analgesic/rapidly penetrates into CNS/hydrolyzed to morphine/addictive/misused
→ CODEIN ←
  • central affecting antitusic = ↓ bronchial secretion
  • analgesic mixtures
  • Natural occurrence in opium does not cover the requirments
  • therefore it is prepared from morphine via semisynthetic route
→ THEBAINE ←
  • starting reagent for preparation of:
  • CODEINE,HYDROCODONE (VICODIN),
  • non-addictive analgesic ( BUTORPHANOL)
  • addictive OXYCODONE (DINARKON, OXYCONTIN)
  • from THEBAINE also derived ALLYLOXYCODON (NALOXON) = antagonist of morphine
→ PAPAVERINE ←
  • belongs to spasmolytics (↓ tonus of smooth muscle → GIT,Cardiovasular syste,Respiratory & Urinary tract)
  • prepared via synthesis (not enought from opium isolation)
→ NOSCAPINE ← (NARCOTINE)
  • waste product of opium processing
  • central antitussic ( relaxation effects as papaverine)
  • does not trigger addiction so is used instead of codeine
