DIURETICS

I.OVERVIEW

→ induce ↑ urine flow

  • inhibitors of renal ion transporter
  • ↓ reabsorption of Na+ at different sites in the nephron
→ as a result Na+ and other ions
  • Cl- enter the urine in greater than normal amounts along with water
  • carried passively to maintain osomotic equillibrium
→ Diuretics thus
  • ↑ the volume of urine
  • change the pH
  • ionic composition of the urine and blood
→ efficacy of diuretics varies with the ↑ Na+ secretion
  • 2% [weak] → potasium-sparing diuretics
  • 20%[potent] → loop diuretics
  • osmotic diuretics and carbonic anhydrase inhibitors.
    • that prevent water reabsortption , aldosterone antagonist ,
  • used to treat abnormal fluid retention (edema)
  • treating hypertension
    • in which the diuretic ↓ blood volume → ↓ the peripheral resistance → ↓ blood pressure.

II.NORMAL REGULATION OF FLUID AND ELECTROLYTES BY THE KIDNEYS.


→ 16-20% of the blood plasma entering the kideny

  • filtrered from the glomerular capillaries into the Bowman capsule
[filtrate free of proteins and blood cells]
  • but does contain low-weight molecular components
    • glucose
    • sodium bicarbonate
    • amino acids
    • other organic solutes
    • electrolytes (Na,K,Cl)
→ kidney regulates the ionic composition and volume of urine
  • active reabsorption/secretion of ions
  • and/or passive reabsrorption of water at five functional zones of the nephron
1.the proximal convoluted tubule
2.descending loop of Henle
3.ascending loop of Henle
4.distal convoluted tubule
5.collecting tubule and duct

III.KIDNEY FUNCTION IN DISEAE


iii.A.Edematous states

→ amount of sodium chloride reabsorbed by the kidney tubules is abnormal high

  • leads to retention of water
  • ↑ blood volume
  • expansion of the extravascular fluid compartment
    • resulting in edema in tissues

iii.A.1.-Heart failure

→ ↓ ability of the failing heart to sustain adequate cardiac output

  • cause the kidney to respond as if there were a ↓ in blood volume (hypovolemia)
→ kidney
  • retains more salt and water
    • raising blood volume
    • increasing the amount of blood that is returned to the heart
→ diseased heart
  • cannot ↑ output
  • ↑ vascular volume → edema
→ Loop diuretics are commonly used.

iii.A.2.-Hepatic ascites

→ accumulation of fluid in the abdominal cavity

  • cirrhosis of the liver
a.Increased portal blood pressure
→ blood flow in the portal system is blocked in cirrhosis
  • ↑ portal blood pressure
b.Secondary hyperaldosteronism
→ fluid retention is also promoted by ↑ levels of circulating aldosterone due to ↓ blood volume
  • ↓ ability of the liver to inactivate the steroid hormone and → ↑ Na+ and water reabsorption
    • ↑ vascular volume
    • exacerbation of fluid accumulation
→ potassium-sparing diuretic → Spirolactone is effective in this condition

iii.A.3-Nephrotic syndrome

→ after damage

  • glomerural membranes allow plasma proteins to enter the glomerular ultrafiltrate
  • ↓ collodial osmotic pressure
  • resulting in edema
→ low plasma volume
  • stimulate aldosterone secretion [through renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system]
  • leads to retention of Na+ and fluid → which aggravating the edema

iii.A.4-Premanstrual edema

→ associated with mestruation

  • imbalance in hormones
    • esterogen excess → loss of fluid in the extracellular space → diuretics [reduce edema]

iii.B..-Nonedematous states


iii.B.1.-Hypertension

→ Thiazides have been widely used in the treatment of hypertension.

  • ability not only to ↓ blood volume but also to dilate arterioles

iii.B.2.-Hypercalemia

→ loop diuretics are used

  • promote calcium excretion
→ normal saline must also be unfused to maintain blood volume.

iii.B.3-Diabetes inspidus

→ when patients suffers from polyuria and polydipsia

  • usually respond to thiazide diuretics.
→ ↓ plasma volume → drop in glomerular filtration rate and promoting the reabsorption of Na+ and water.

IV.THIAZIDES AND RELATED AGENTS

→ Sulfonamides derivatives

  • related structure to carbonic anhydrase inhibitors
→ greater diuretic activity than acetazolamide
  • they act on the kidney by different mechanims
→ affect the distal tubule
  • all have equal maxium diuretic effect
    • differing only in potency.
→ called "celling diuretics"
  • ↑ the dose above normal
    • does not promote a further diuretic response.
→ like loop diuretics and thiazide actions partly depend on renal prostagladin syntheis

IV.A.Clorothiazide

→ first modern diuretic

  • orally active
  • affecting the severe edema of cirrhosis and heart failure with min. side effects

Hydrochlorothiazide and Chlorothalidone

→ now used more commonly
→ Hydrochlorothiazide

  • far less ability to inhibit carbonic anhydrase compared to chlorothiazide.
  • it is also more potent.
  • required dose is considerably lower than that of chlorothiazide
→ on the other hand
  • efficacy is exactly the same
[chlorthalidone,indapamide,metolazone reffered as thiazide-like diuretics because they contain sulfonamide residue in their chemical structures and their mechanism of aciton is similar]-[they are not truly thiazides]

IV.A.1.-Mechanism of action

→ act mainly in the cortical region of the

  • ascending loop of Henle
  • distal tubule
    • to ↓ reabsorption of Na+
      • by inhibition of a Na+/Cl- co-transporter of the luminal membrane of the tubules.
→ less effect in the proximal tube.
  • ↑ conc. of Na+ and Cl- in the tubular fluid,
→ acid-base balance is not usually affected.
[because the site of action of thiazides derivatives is on the luminal membrane ; with '↓ renal function , thiazide diuretics lose efficacy.]

IV.A.2.-Actions

a.Increased excretion of Na+ and Cl-

  • cause diuresis with ↑ Na+ and Cl- exrection → excrection of avery hyperosmolar urine.
b.Loss of K+
  • because thiazides ↑ the Na in the filtrate arriving at the distal tube
    • more K+ is also exchanged for Na+
      • resulting in the continual loss of K+ from the body with prolonged use of drugs
c.Loss of Mg+
  • Mg deficiency requring supplementation which can occur with chronic use of thiazides diuretics
    • particulary in elderly patients
d.Deacreased urinary calcium excretion
  • ↓ Ca+ content urine by promoting the reabsorption of Ca+
[the effects contrasts with the loop diuretic,which ↑ the Ca+ conc. of the urine.]
e.Reduce peripheral vascular resistance
  • initial reduction in blood pressure → ↓ blood volume → ↓ cardiac output
  • continued hypotensive effect resulted from ↓ periphera; vascular resistance.

IV.A.3.-Therapeutic uses


iv.A.3.a.-Hypertension

→ mainstay of hypertention medicaiton
→ ↓ systolic and diastolic blood pressure

  • for mild/moderate hypertention
→ blood pressure stabilized at lower level and can be maintained by daily dose
  • causing a low peripheral resistance without having a major diuretic effect.

iv.A.3.b.-Heart failure

→ loop diuretics

  • choice of ↓ extracellular volume in heart failure

iv.A.3.c.-Hypercalciuria

→ treating idiopathic hypercalciuria

  • inhibit urinary Ca+2 excrection
→ particular benifit in patients with calcium oxalate stones.

iv.A.4.-Pharmacokinetics

→ are effective orally
→ 1 to 3 weeks to produce a stable reduction in blood pressure

  • exhibit a prolonged biologic half-life.
→ all thiazides secreted by the organic acid secretory syste of the kidney.

IV.5.Adverse effects

→ fluid and electrolyte balance

IV.5.a.potassium depletion

→ can predispose patients with digoxin treatment to ventricular arrythmias
→ K+ can be supplemented by diet alone

  • ↑ intake of citrus frutis , bananas and prunes.
→ activation of renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system
  • by ↓ in intravascular volume → K+ losses.
→ K+ deficiency can be overcome by
  • spirolactone [which interferes with aldosteorne aciton]
  • or by admin. of triamterene or amiloride
    • act to retain K+
→ low-sodium diet blunt the potassium depletion caused by thiazide diuretics

IV.5.b-hyponatremia

→may develop due to

  • elevation of ADH as a result of hypovolemia
  • diminished diluting capactiy of the kidney and ↑ thirst.
→ limiting water intake and ↓ the dose of diuretic

IV.5.c.-hyperuricemia

→ thiazides ↑ serum uric acid by ↓ the amount of acid excreted by the organic acid secretory system
→ insoluble,uric acid deposits in the joints

IV.5.d.-volume depletion

→ orthostatic hypotention / light-headaeches

IV.5.e.hypercalcemia

→ inhibit the secretion of Ca2+ → elevated levels of Ca2+ in the blood

IV.5.f.hyperglycemia

→ due to impaired release of insulin and tissue uptake of glucose.

IV.5.g.hyperlipidemia

→ can cause 5-15% ↑ in serum cholesterol

  • as well as ↑ serum low density lipoproteins.
→ lipid levels may return to normal with long-term therapy
